## Executive Summary

This document reviews professional liability considerations for Pixelated Empathy as a therapeutic AI platform. The review addresses legal risks, insurance requirements, disclaimers, scope of practice, and risk mitigation strategies.

## Scope of Service and Disclaimers

### Service Classification

**Pixelated Empathy is:**
- ? A therapeutic AI support tool
- ? An educational and self-help resource
- ? A supplement to professional mental health care
- ? A crisis detection and referral system

**Pixelated Empathy is NOT:**
- ? A replacement for professional mental health treatment
- ? A licensed mental health professional
- ? A provider of medical or clinical diagnoses
- ? A substitute for emergency mental health services
- ? A crisis hotline or emergency response service

### User Disclaimers

#### Primary Disclaimer
```
Pixelated Empathy provides AI-powered therapeutic support and is not a 
substitute for professional mental health care. The platform is designed 
to supplement, not replace, licensed mental health treatment. Users should 
seek professional help for serious mental health conditions, emergencies, 
or when in crisis.

The AI responses are generated based on psychological principles and 
research, but they are not professional diagnoses, treatment plans, or 
clinical recommendations. Users retain responsibility for their mental 
health decisions and should consult licensed professionals for clinical care.
```

#### Crisis Disclaimer
```
If you are experiencing a mental health emergency or crisis, please:
1. Call 911 or your local emergency services
2. Contact the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988
3. Go to your nearest emergency room
4. Contact your mental health professional immediately

Pixelated Empathy's crisis detection features are designed to identify 
potential crises and facilitate professional referrals, but immediate 
emergency situations require direct contact with emergency services.
```

#### Professional Disclaimer
```
Pixelated Empathy does not provide medical advice, clinical diagnoses, 
or treatment recommendations. All content is for informational and 
supportive purposes only. Users should always consult qualified mental 
health professionals for clinical assessment, diagnosis, and treatment.
```

## Incident Response

### Professional Liability Incidents

**Definition**: Any incident that may result in a claim of professional negligence, malpractice, or failure to provide adequate care.

**Response Procedures:**
1. **Immediate Assessment** (0-2 hours)
   - Document incident details
   - Assess potential liability
   - Notify legal counsel
   - Preserve all relevant evidence

2. **Investigation** (2-24 hours)
   - Review system logs and records
   - Interview relevant personnel
   - Document timeline and facts
   - Assess scope of potential impact

3. **Notification** (Within 24 hours)
   - Notify insurance carrier
   - Consult with legal counsel
   - Prepare incident report
   - Notify management team

4. **Remediation** (Ongoing)
   - Address immediate safety concerns
   - Implement corrective actions
   - Update policies and procedures
   - Monitor for similar incidents

### Documentation Requirements

- Incident reports maintained for 7 years

- Evidence preservation procedures
- Legal hold procedures
